Patrol Leader

When I became Patrol Leader of the Savage Avocados, our patrol was considered the least competitive. Most members were middle schoolers, and motivation was low. I re‑designed meetings around engagement: skill‑based games like First‑Aid Jeopardy and Navigation Relay Races, a shared music playlist to build culture, and clear goals we celebrated together (dessert runs after rank‑ups).

The results showed up fast: higher participation, more merit badges, and better competition scores. The next year we saw 18 total promotions in my patrol. That experience taught me that effective leadership isn’t about pressure—it’s about sparking motivation and giving people ways to succeed together.
